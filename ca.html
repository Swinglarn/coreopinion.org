A country at a crossroads

The 2025 election was shaped by one question above all others: how should Canada respond to American tariffs and threats to sovereignty? Mark Carney won on economic nationalism. Pierre Poilievre lost on a platform built for a different moment. The political map shifted under everyone.

Six parties, not two

Canadian politics has genuine multi-party competition. The Bloc speaks only to Quebec voters, the NDP pulls from the left, and the PPC pulls from a very different right than the Conservatives occupy. Your result maps your views across all six and may surprise you.
